The Canberra Air Disaster

A TRUE STORY

1940

It's all about speed.

At 99 knots the Lockheed Hudson bomber, even with its high wing loading, is unlikely to stall.

This is basic training.

But when Hudson A16-97 approached Canberra one day in 1940, it was travelling too slow.

Witnesses saw the bomber circle, bank steeply, drop one wing, and then pancake into the ground.

After that - fire.

ten people

Lives extinguished faster than the fire on a match head in a breeze.

Their spirits now haunt the pine forests and lonely dirt roads that lead to the monolith in their memorial.

1971

On February 26, 1971, Keren Rowland, a pregnant 20 year old girl planned to travel to Civic for a party.

However, due to a change of plan, Keren drove four miles by herself, with her Mini Morris 850 sedan.
But she never arrived.

Her car was found a few days later.
no more gas in the tank, and the interior was also abandoned — no trace of Keren.

It wasn't until sevral weeks later that all questions about Keren’s whereabouts would come to the final answer.

May 13 was a beautiful and sunny day in Canberra.
A university lecturer decided to visit the Air Disaster Memorial to find a sunny spot to read.

It wouldn’t take long until she found lost belongings, scattered about on the ground,
only to stumble upon the dead body of Keren with her unborn child.

While her cause of death isn’t established, there were fractures on her body indicating she had been strangled.

...

Her ghost has been seen standing on the edge of a clearing,
a brown sepia image that people have described accurately as her,
even down to such details as having her hair pulled back, something she rarely did, but had done so on the day of her death.

2000

It’s 10:00 pm.

Casie and her three friends have pulled into a small clearing in a dense pine forest.

They’re here to see what all the fuss is about.

They parked up and left the car running with the lights on.
After sitting there for a couple of minutes, the engine just stopped.

At first, they didn’t think anything of it.

The lights were still on, and they had a torch in the back of the car, so they went to see if they could work out what had happened.

It was quiet at first, then all of a sudden it sounded like a few girls screaming and crying in the distance.
At this point, they all freaked out and jumped back in the car, desperately trying to start it.

...

This went on for about 10 minutes.

Eventually, the car started and they left.

Casie and her friends have never been back since.
